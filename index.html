Let's Talk About Volcanoes

...or more specifically, volcanic activity.

There are 55 different types of volcanic events, ranging from ash clouds to island formation. But which of these are the most common? And which one's the deadliest? Let's have a look.

Of the 10,761 recorded events, 7,825 were explosions, making this the most common type of volcanic activity.

Explosions are also the most fatal type of volcanic activity - constituting 411 of the 585 events with fatalities.

Earthquakes, which are the most common form of volcanic activity not involving lava or ash, have an extremely high fatality rate - around 15% of all volcanically induced earthquakes have at least one casualty.

NO NEED TO LOOK SO ASHEN

Volcanoes may be dangerous but there are still ways to safely visit an active volcano.

Only 520 volcanoes have been active in the last 2,000 years - this leaves 127 dormant or extinct volcanes that can be visited safely!

Do your research! Stratovolcanoes, particularly those with Andesite formations, tend to be the most lethal. There are 294 other volcanoes that do not fit into this category.

Of the 585 fatal events, 22 were preceded by either ashfall or ash plumes. Ash is generally considered a warning sign, and volcanoes with recent ash are usually avoided by tour guides.
